About

"A cryptocurrency wallet is a software program that stores private and public keys and interacts with various blockchain to enable users to send and receive digital currency and monitor their balance. If you want to use Bitcoin or any other cryptocurrency, you will need to have a digital wallet." - Ameer Rosic

On this page we will be talking about the pros and cons of using a Desktop, Online/ Cloud, Mobile, Paper, and Hardware Wallets. ALWAYS BACKUP WALLETS. KEEP SOFTWARE UPDATED. TWO FACTOR AUTHENTICATION. Do you need a wallet for everyday purchases or just buying and holding currency? Do you plan to use several currencies or one single currency? Do you require access to your digital wallets from anywhere or only from home?

Versioning is a huge part of wallet selection. Based on expected cryptocurrency usage needs a user will prefer certain types of wallets over others.

Versions of Cryptocurrency Wallets

This video above goes into the pros and cons of the multiple different kinds of cryptocurrency wallets. Characterists to consider when choosing a wallet:

  • Accessability: User Interface/ Ease of Use/ Speed on Transactions.
  • Security: Is the wallet online "hot" or offline "cold" - can only access it physically.
  • Variety of Cryptocurrencies: Only Bitcoin or Litecoin, Bitcoin Cash, and Ethereum.

Coinbase Explanation and Tutorial

Coinbase has recently risen as the top app in the iOS store and become the most popular wallet for users. Its simple interface and display or cryptocurrency values streamline the process. Users can currently buy/sell Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Litecoin. Coinbase will soon support Bitcoin Cash.
